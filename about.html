Meet Your Teacher

Kriti Sharma

Founder & Lead Educator · Flourish Phoenix

I am an educator, researcher, and lifelong learner with a deep belief that curiosity — not compliance — is what makes a student thrive. I have spent five years teaching in wildly different environments, and what I have learned above all else is that kids flourish when someone genuinely believes in them.

I bring an intersectional lens, a love of reading and mathematics, and a commitment to mental health and identity-affirming education to everything I do. I am fluent in four languages and have taught across three countries.

What Drives Me

The things I think about constantly.

Reading & Literature
Books are how I think.

From Shakespeare to Allen Ginsberg, literature is the lens I use to understand identity, power, and the human experience. I want students to love reading — not because they have to, but because stories are how we make sense of the world.

Mathematics & Curiosity
Math is a language, too.

I love mathematics for the same reason I love poetry — both require you to sit with a problem, look at it from unexpected angles, and find elegant solutions. Curiosity is the skill that underlies both.

Mental Health & Identity
The whole student matters.

I care deeply about psychology, mental health, gender and sexuality, and how identity shapes the way students experience school. Education that ignores who a student is will never reach who they could become.

Community & Arts
Art as infrastructure.

My background includes nonprofit community outreach and funding for independent artists. I believe creative expression and community connection are not extras — they are foundations.

Research & Writing
Uplifting marginalized voices.

My academic work aims to amplify perspectives that mainstream scholarship has historically overlooked. I bring that same commitment to Flourish — building a school where every student sees themselves reflected.

Languages
Four languages, one classroom.

Speaking English, Hindi, Japanese, and Spanish has shaped how I think about communication, context, and the way meaning shifts across cultures. Multilingualism is a superpower, and I want students to know that.
